HISTORY

Imperial Foundation
Imperial Foundation

Petrópolis, the "Imperial City", was founded by order of Dom Pedro II on March 16, 1843. The emperor, seeking refuge from the intense heat of Rio de Janeiro, chose the lands of the former Córrego Seco Farm to build his summer palace and an agricultural colony.

Dom Pedro II
1843
Imperial Apogee
Imperial Apogee

During the Second Empire, Petrópolis reached its peak as the political and cultural center of the Empire. The Imperial Palace became the official summer residence of the royal family, attracting the country's political and aristocratic elite. The city was known as the "Brazilian Versailles" due to its sophistication.

Princess Isabel
1870
Contemporary Petropolis
Contemporary Petropolis

Today, Petrópolis keeps its historical heritage alive while developing as an important tourist, technological and educational center. The Historic Center, listed by IPHAN, preserves the original layout and buildings from the imperial period, attracting visitors from all over the world.
